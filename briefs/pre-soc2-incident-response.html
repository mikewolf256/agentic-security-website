  • "Show us your incident detection and response process"

This won't pass audit. And you can't afford to hire a full SOC team.

Common Use Cases

1. IDOR Account Enumeration (Fintech)

The Attack: User probes `/api/loans/12345`, `12346`, `12347` to find unauthorized accounts.

Traditional Detection: Flags anyone accessing 3+ sequential IDs → False positives on legitimate multi-loan users.

Ownership-Aware Detection: Only alerts if user accesses OTHER users' resources → 90% reduction in false positives.

2. Alert Fatigue for Engineering Teams

Before: 100+ alerts/day from WAF/observability tooling. 80% false positives. Engineering spends 2-3 hours/day triaging.

After: 10-20 validated alerts/day. 95%+ are real threats. Engineering saves 20+ hours/week.

3. Auditor Evidence Package

SOC2 Requirement (CC7.2): "The entity monitors system components...and evaluates security events to determine whether they could impact the achievement of the entity's objectives."

What You Show: Complete audit trail, detection metrics, incident response timeline, MITRE ATT&CK mappings, automated response playbooks.

FAQ for Leadership

Q: Do we need to hire security analysts?

No. We provide the first layer of triage. High-confidence threats are handled automatically. Medium-confidence alerts are validated by our team before escalation. You make final decisions on complex scenarios.

Q: What about customer data and PII?

All PII is scrubbed locally (redaction + regex/NER) before any external API call. Customer emails, IPs, credit cards never leave your infrastructure. GDPR/PCI-DSS compliant by design.

Q: Can we run this on-premise?

Yes. Docker/Kubernetes deployment in your VPC. All sensitive data stays in your infrastructure. Only scrubbed, anonymized telemetry hits external AI APIs for analysis.

Q: What if we grow and need a full SOC later?

Perfect. The middleware stays in place and your SOC analysts get higher-quality, pre-validated alerts. We become your Tier 1 automation, they become Tier 2/3 investigation.
